# Compensation Governance

This document summarizes core compensation metrics and governance concepts used in HR Data and AI analytics work. The aim is to support fair, controlled, and explainable pay decisions.

## Salary Bands

### Plain English Meaning

Salary bands define the minimum, midpoint, and maximum pay range for a job level or role family.

### Formula

Not a ratio by itself. A band is typically represented as:

Band = Minimum, Midpoint, Maximum

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

Bands create structure for consistent pay decisions, promotion guidelines, and market alignment.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

Salary bands help forecast labor cost, control exception decisions, and maintain consistency across business units.

## Compa-Ratio

### Plain English Meaning

Compa-ratio shows how an employee's salary compares with the midpoint of their salary band.

### Formula

Compa-Ratio = Employee Salary / Salary Band Midpoint

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It helps reward teams assess whether employees are paid below, near, or above policy position.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It shows how current pay compares with target pay positioning and helps estimate cost of market alignment actions.

## Range Penetration

### Plain English Meaning

Range penetration shows where an employee's salary sits between the minimum and maximum of the band.

### Formula

Range Penetration = (Employee Salary - Band Minimum) / (Band Maximum - Band Minimum)

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It helps assess progression within a range and supports decisions on merit increases and promotions.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It gives leaders a clearer view of salary maturity than midpoint comparison alone.

## Market Index

### Plain English Meaning

Market index shows how company pay compares with external market benchmark data.

### Formula

Market Index = Internal Reference Salary / External Market Benchmark

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It helps determine if pay is competitive in critical skill areas and locations.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It informs retention risk, hiring competitiveness, and budget planning.

## Benchmark Gap

### Plain English Meaning

Benchmark gap shows the difference between actual pay and a chosen benchmark such as midpoint or market median.

### Formula

Benchmark Gap = Employee Salary - Benchmark Salary

or as a percentage:

Benchmark Gap Percentage = (Employee Salary - Benchmark Salary) / Benchmark Salary

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It helps identify underpaid or overpaid populations and supports corrective actions.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It quantifies the financial impact of market adjustments or harmonization programs.

## Pay Equity Indicators

### Plain English Meaning

Pay equity indicators are measures used to identify whether comparable employee groups may be paid differently after considering relevant factors.

### Formula

No single universal formula. Common indicators include adjusted pay gap percentages, gender pay gap views, and unexplained variance after controlling for role, level, and geography.

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It supports fairness, compliance, and trust in pay practices.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It helps leaders prioritize remediation, manage legal risk, and support transparency.

## Employees Below Band Minimum

### Plain English Meaning

This shows how many employees are paid below the minimum of their assigned salary band.

### Formula

Employees Below Minimum = Count of employees where salary < band minimum

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It flags policy exceptions, possible data errors, or populations that need salary correction.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It estimates required budget to bring employees into policy range.

## Employees Above Band Maximum

### Plain English Meaning

This shows how many employees are paid above the maximum of their assigned salary band.

### Formula

Employees Above Maximum = Count of employees where salary > band maximum

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It highlights red-circle cases, legacy pay situations, or role-band mismatches.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It supports risk review, succession planning, and future pay action decisions.

## Compensation Budget Utilization

### Plain English Meaning

Compensation budget utilization shows how much of the approved compensation budget has been used.

### Formula

Compensation Budget Utilization = Actual Spend / Approved Budget

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

It helps teams control merit, bonus, and market-adjustment spending.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

It gives leadership visibility into affordability, remaining budget, and forecast pressure.

## Governance Risks

### Plain English Meaning

Governance risks are conditions that reduce consistency, fairness, or auditability in pay decisions.

### Formula

Not formula-based. This is a control area monitored through indicators and exception reporting.

### Why Comp and Ben Cares

Reward teams need to ensure pay actions follow policy and can be explained.

### How It Supports Finance and Leadership

Strong governance reduces compliance risk, financial surprises, and leadership escalations.

### Common Governance Risks

- Employees assigned to the wrong salary band
- Midpoints not updated after market refresh
- Off-cycle pay changes with weak approval evidence
- Inconsistent currency conversion rules
- Incentive payouts not aligned to approved rules
- Limited visibility into pay equity exceptions
