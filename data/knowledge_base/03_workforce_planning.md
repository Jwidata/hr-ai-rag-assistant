# Workforce Planning

This document covers core workforce-planning measures and scenario concepts used to support leadership decisions on cost, capacity, and organization design.

## Workforce Cost

### Simple Explanation

Workforce cost is the total cost of employees over a period. It usually includes base salary, bonus, employer taxes, and benefits. Some organizations also include contractors separately.

### Formula or Logic

Workforce Cost = Sum of salary + incentives + employer on-costs + benefits

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

How much are we spending on the workforce by business unit, geography, or function?

## Workforce Cost per FTE

### Simple Explanation

This shows the average workforce cost for one full-time equivalent employee.

### Formula or Logic

Workforce Cost per FTE = Total Workforce Cost / Total FTE

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

Which functions or countries have a higher cost structure per unit of workforce capacity?

## Budget Variance

### Simple Explanation

Budget variance measures the difference between actual workforce cost and budgeted workforce cost.

### Formula or Logic

Budget Variance = Actual Workforce Cost - Budgeted Workforce Cost

Budget Variance Percentage = (Actual - Budget) / Budget

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

Are we overspending or underspending versus workforce budget, and where is the gap coming from?

## Forecasted Workforce Cost

### Simple Explanation

Forecasted workforce cost estimates future labor spend based on current staffing, planned hires, exits, salary changes, and organizational decisions.

### Formula or Logic

Forecast = Current Run Rate + Planned Hires + Planned Salary Changes - Expected Exits + Other Known Changes

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

What will our workforce cost look like by year end if current hiring and attrition trends continue?

## Hiring Scenario

### Simple Explanation

A hiring scenario models what happens to capacity and cost if the business hires more slowly, faster, or in different locations or role types.

### Formula or Logic

Scenario logic compares planned headcount and cost under different assumptions for hiring volume, hiring timing, and role mix.

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

What is the cost and delivery impact if we fill only 70 percent of planned roles this quarter?

## Attrition Replacement Cost

### Simple Explanation

Attrition replacement cost estimates the financial impact of replacing employees who leave.

### Formula or Logic

Replacement Cost = Number of Replacements x Average Cost per Replacement

Average replacement cost may include recruiting, onboarding, lost productivity, and training.

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

What is the hidden business cost of attrition in critical roles?

## Salary Increase Impact

### Simple Explanation

Salary increase impact estimates how a merit cycle, market adjustment, or targeted pay action changes workforce cost.

### Formula or Logic

Salary Increase Impact = Sum of proposed increase amounts across in-scope employees

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

What is the annualized cost if we apply a 3 percent increase to one population and a 5 percent increase to another?

## Org Design Scenario Modelling

### Simple Explanation

Org design scenario modelling tests how different structures affect cost, spans, layers, and management load.

### Formula or Logic

Typical logic compares current and target structures using measures such as manager ratio, span of control, layers, and workforce cost.

### Example Leadership Question It Answers

What happens to cost and manager effectiveness if we reduce one management layer or centralize a support function?

## Good Practice Notes

Strong workforce planning usually combines:

- Headcount and FTE views
- Position and vacancy data
- Cost forecasts
- Attrition assumptions
- Scenario comparison
- Clear linkage to business strategy
